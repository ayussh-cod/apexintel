# Garry Kasparov — Actionable Tasks

> Derived from the strategy of Garry Kasparov in professional chess.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Week 1-2)
- [ ] Analyze 5 classic Kasparov games, focusing on opening principles.
- [ ] Solve 10 tactical puzzles daily for 30 minutes to improve calculation.
- [ ] Study common opening theory: center control, piece development, king safety.
- [ ] Review basic endgame techniques, specifically King and Pawn vs. King.
- [ ] Play 3 slow-time control games (30+ min) per week, then analyze with an engine.

## Phase 2 — Build (Week 3-6)
- [ ] Deep dive into Kasparov's favored openings (e.g., King's Indian Defense, Ruy Lopez).
- [ ] Practice specific middlegame themes like pawn structures and piece coordination.
- [ ] Conduct detailed post-mortem analysis of own games, identifying 3 key errors.
- [ ] Study 20 annotated master games, focusing on strategic ideas and plans.
- [ ] Play 5 longer-time control games (60+ min) per week on an online platform.

## Phase 3 — Compound (Month 2-3)
- [ ] Develop a personalized opening repertoire for both White and Black.
- [ ] Participate in an online rated tournament (e.g., rapid or blitz).
- [ ] Study psychological aspects of competitive chess, such as time management.
- [ ] Review historical matches of Kasparov, identifying how he exploited opponents' weaknesses.
- [ ] Create a personal database of tactical motifs and strategic patterns from own games.

## Daily / Weekly Rituals
- [ ] Daily: Solve 30 minutes of tactical puzzles.
- [ ] Daily: Review 1-2 lines of chosen opening repertoire.
- [ ] Weekly: Analyze one of my own games thoroughly for mistakes and improvements.
- [ ] Weekly: Study an annotated master game, focusing on a specific theme.
