# Anatoly Karpov — Actionable Tasks

> Derived from the strategy of Anatoly Karpov in professional chess.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Week 1-2)
- [ ] Analyze 5 classic Anatoly Karpov games, focusing on positional understanding.
- [ ] Study basic endgame theory: King and Pawn vs. King, and simple Rook endgames.
- [ ] Solve 10 tactical puzzles daily, prioritizing basic forks, pins, and skewers.
- [ ] Review opening principles for 3 chosen stable openings (e.g., Caro-Kann Defense, Queen's Gambit Declined).
- [ ] Play 3 slow-time control games online, annotating own mistakes and decision-making.
- [ ] Read a chapter on pawn structures and weaknesses from a foundational chess book.

## Phase 2 — Build (Week 3-6)
- [ ] Deep dive into a specific Karpov-esque opening repertoire, mastering key lines up to 10 moves.
- [ ] Practice 20 complex tactical combinations from a dedicated puzzle book or online platform.
- [ ] Study a specific middlegame theme like prophylaxis, minority attack, or piece exchanges.
- [ ] Analyze own tournament or serious online games to identify recurring weaknesses and patterns.
- [ ] Play 5 slow-time control games with a stronger player or coach, requesting post-game feedback.
- [ ] Develop a personalized game annotation system to track critical moments and thought processes.

## Phase 3 — Compound (Month 2-3)
- [ ] Participate in a rated tournament, applying learned strategies and analyzing every game played.
- [ ] Review all games played in the last month, categorizing mistakes by phase (opening, middlegame, endgame).
- [ ] Develop a personalized pre-game and post-game routine to optimize focus and recovery.
- [ ] Study advanced endgame concepts such as fortress positions and distant passed pawns.
- [ ] Analyze a new opening variation or sub-variation to broaden repertoire and surprise opponents.
- [ ] Practice blindfold chess for 30 minutes twice a week to improve visualization skills.
- [ ] Read a master-level game collection, focusing on strategic ideas and game conversion.

## Daily / Weekly Rituals
- [ ] Solve 15-20 tactical puzzles focusing on accuracy and speed daily.
- [ ] Review a key opening line or variation from your repertoire daily.
- [ ] Analyze one complete game (own or master) every other day.
- [ ] Meditate for 10 minutes before playing serious games or study sessions.
- [ ] Engage in 30 minutes of physical exercise at least 3 times a week to maintain stamina.
