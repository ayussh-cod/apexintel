# Jose Raul Capablanca — Actionable Tasks

> Derived from the strategy of Jose Raul Capablanca in professional chess.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Week 1-2)
- [ ] Understand and apply basic opening principles (center control, rapid development, king safety).
- [ ] Memorize common tactical motifs: forks, pins, skewers, discovered attacks, back rank mates.
- [ ] Solve at least 20 simple tactical puzzles daily, aiming for high accuracy.
- [ ] Study 3-5 Capablanca's early games to observe his clear and logical approach.
- [ ] Learn fundamental endgame concepts, starting with King and Pawn vs. King.
- [ ] Select and learn the first 5-7 moves of one reliable opening for White and one for Black.

## Phase 2 — Build (Week 3-6)
- [ ] Analyze your own games to identify recurring errors in all phases (opening, middlegame, endgame).
- [ ] Play 3-5 slow time control games weekly, focusing on applying learned principles, not just winning.
- [ ] Study middlegame strategy: pawn structures, weak squares, piece activity, space advantage.
- [ ] Deepen understanding of your chosen openings by studying common middlegame positions arising from them.
- [ ] Practice calculating combinations up to 3-4 moves deep from various positions.
- [ ] Read a foundational chess strategy book (e.g., "Logical Chess: Move By Move" by Chernev).

## Phase 3 — Compound (Month 2-3)
- [ ] Participate in at least one online classical tournament or OTB club event.
- [ ] Refine your opening repertoire by learning key variations and common plans for each side.
- [ ] Work on advanced endgame theory, focusing on Rook Endgames and Minor Piece Endgames.
- [ ] Study master games specifically for the decision-making process in complex positions.
- [ ] Utilize a chess engine to deeply analyze your most critical games, understanding engine's suggested moves.
- [ ] Practice blindfold chess for 5-10 minutes daily to improve visualization skills.

## Daily / Weekly Rituals
- [ ] Daily: Solve 10-15 tactical puzzles from a respected source (e.g., Lichess, Chess.com tactics trainer).
- [ ] Daily: Review one short (under 30 moves) master game to absorb new ideas and patterns.
- [ ] Weekly: Play at least two long-format (30+ minutes per side) games.
- [ ] Weekly: Analyze one of your own games without an engine first, then use an engine to verify and correct.
- [ ] Weekly: Dedicate 30 minutes to reviewing opening theory for your repertoire.

## Metrics to Track

|-------------------------|---------------------------|------------|

| Opening Preparation (%) | >70% adherence to repertoire | Per game   |
