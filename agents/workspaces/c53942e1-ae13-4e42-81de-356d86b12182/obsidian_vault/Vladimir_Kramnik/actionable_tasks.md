# Vladimir Kramnik — Actionable Tasks

> Derived from the strategy of Vladimir Kramnik in professional chess.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Week 1-2)
- [ ] Research and acquire a beginner's guide to chess history from reliable sources.
- [ ] Begin reading about the early history of chess, focusing on key historical figures and their contributions.
- [ ] Locate and download PGN files or obtain books featuring games by François-André Danican Philidor.
- [ ] Play through at least 5 annotated games by Philidor, noting key strategic ideas related to pawn structure and piece coordination.
- [ ] Identify and source a collection of games by Adolf Anderssen and Paul Morphy for future study.
- [ ] Dedicate 30 minutes daily to studying the early games of chess masters, absorbing their styles.

## Phase 2 — Build (Week 3-6)
- [ ] Focus studies on 20th-century chess players mentioned by Kramnik, such as Karpov, Petrosian, and Kasparov.
- [ ] Analyze at least 10 games from Anatoly Karpov, specifically focusing on his positional play and prophylactic moves.
- [ ] Study 10 games from Garry Kasparov, paying close attention to his dynamic play, king safety, and initiative.
- [ ] Research the key strategic themes and contributions of Tigran Petrosian, particularly his defensive and prophylactic concepts.
- [ ] Play through 5 games of Petrosian, specifically looking for how he stifles opponent's plans.
- [ ] Compile a list of personal strategic takeaways from the games of Karpov, Kasparov, and Petrosian.

## Phase 3 — Compound (Month 2-3)
- [ ] Integrate historical chess knowledge into personal game analysis sessions, comparing your play to master principles.
- [ ] Annotate at least 3 of your own games, referencing historical precedents or master techniques observed.
- [ ] Create a personal opening repertoire inspired by a blend of historical masters and modern professional players.
- [ ] Participate in online chess tournaments, consciously attempting to apply learned historical and modern strategies.
- [ ] Review annotated games of current top professional players, comparing their styles with classical and modern era strategies.
- [ ] Write a short reflective essay on the evolution of chess strategy from Philidor through the 20th-century champions.

## Daily / Weekly Rituals
- [ ] Daily: Review 1-2 annotated master games from different historical eras (classical, romantic, modern).
- [ ] Daily: Solve 5-10 tactical puzzles, aiming for speed and accuracy.
- [ ] Weekly: Analyze one of your own games without engine assistance initially, identifying mistakes and learning points.
- [ ] Weekly: Read a chapter from a classic chess book or biography of a chess master.
