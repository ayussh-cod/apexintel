# Jesse Livermore — Strategy Overview

## Background
Jesse Livermore was a legendary stock and commodity trader of the early 20th century, renowned for making and losing multiple fortunes throughout his career. Born in 1877, he began his trading journey at age 14 as a quotation board boy, quickly developing a profound understanding of market dynamics without formal higher education. He famously profited by shorting stocks during the market crashes of 1907 and 1929, cementing his legacy as a master of market timing and trend recognition.

## Core Strategy
Livermore's core strategy centered on [[Trend Following]] and [[Market Psychology]], believing that markets move in discernible trends driven by human sentiment rather than fundamentals alone. He advocated for extreme patience, waiting for specific [[Pivotal Points]] to enter trades only when the market explicitly confirmed his thesis. His approach emphasized letting profitable trades run while cutting losses quickly, utilizing rigorous observation of [[Price Action]] and [[Volume]] to identify and ride market movements rather than predicting them.

## Key Principles
*   **Recognize and Ride Trends**: Focus on identifying and aligning with existing market trends, understanding that the market's price action is the ultimate authority.
*   **Pivotal Point Entry**: Execute trades only at clear [[Pivotal Points]] when the market confirms the directional bias, employing a buffer for entry.
*   **Price and Volume Analysis**: Rely heavily on [[Price Action]] to gauge market sentiment and use [[Volume]] to confirm the strength of price movements.
*   **Market Psychology**: Understand and leverage crowd behavior, as markets frequently overreact, creating opportunities for astute traders.
*   **Disciplined Risk Management**: Implement strict [[Position Sizing]], utilize [[Stop-Loss]] orders, and allow profits to run while diligently managing losing positions.
*   **Patience and Observation**: Exercise patience, waiting for optimal setups and letting the market dictate actions, thereby avoiding [[Overtrading]].

## Unique Edge
Livermore's unique edge lay in his profound understanding of [[Market Psychology]] and his fearless conviction to trade against the prevailing sentiment when his analysis indicated a shift. He developed a highly disciplined system based on keen observation and empirical data, rather than relying on conventional wisdom or formal education. This allowed him to profit from major market downturns, like those in 1907 and 1929, by recognizing underlying trends and applying rigorous risk management long before such approaches were widely adopted.

## Lessons for Practitioners
*   Prioritize recognizing and riding established market trends, rather than attempting to predict future price movements or acting on opinions.
*   Develop a deep understanding of [[Market Psychology]] to anticipate crowd behavior and identify opportunities created by market overreactions.
*   Practice unwavering discipline by executing trades only when the market confirms your thesis at identifiable [[Pivotal Points]], and always manage your risk with [[Stop-Loss]] orders.
